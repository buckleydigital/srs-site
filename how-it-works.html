Our short survey collects just enough information to match you with the right installer. No login, no credit card, no lengthy forms.

We match you with your #1 local installer

Based on your postcode, we identify the highest-rated, CEC-accredited solar installer operating in your area. Our matching considers:

  • Clean Energy Council (CEC) accreditation — non-negotiable
  • Customer reviews and track record in your suburb
  • Experience with homes similar to yours in your area
  • Response time and service standards

You're matched with one installer, not a dozen. This keeps things simple, avoids the chaos of multiple simultaneous sales calls, and means the installer knows they've earned the lead — not just bought it from a pile of 10.

🏆 Why one installer, not three?

Most comparison sites send your details to 3–5 companies at once. You end up fielding calls all week from competing salespeople. We think that's stressful and counterproductive. Our model: one vetted, top-rated local installer contacts you. If you want more quotes, our Quote Checklist helps you request and compare them independently.

The installer reaches out to arrange your free assessment

A local, CEC-accredited installer will contact you — by phone or email, depending on your preference — to introduce themselves and arrange a free, no-obligation site assessment.

This isn't a sales call. It's a genuine conversation about your home, your energy usage, and whether solar is a good fit for your situation. A good installer will tell you honestly if solar isn't right for you — and ours are selected because they do exactly that.

  • No pressure to commit on the first call
  • They'll ask about your roof, shading, switchboard, and usage
  • They'll answer any questions you have upfront
  • You choose a time for the site visit that suits you
4
🏠
Free · No Obligation

Free site assessment at your home

The installer visits your home (or conducts a detailed satellite assessment for remote properties) to design a system specific to your roof, usage, and budget. Here's what to expect:

  • Roof inspection: Orientation, pitch, shading from trees or neighbouring buildings, structural condition, and available area
  • Switchboard check: Assessing capacity, age, and whether an upgrade is needed
  • Usage analysis: They may ask to see a recent electricity bill to size the system correctly
  • System design: Panel placement, inverter selection, string layout, metering requirements
  • Rebate calculation: Your exact STC rebate based on system size, location, and current STC price

💡 What to prepare before the visit

Have a recent electricity bill handy (shows your kWh usage and tariff). Note which parts of your roof get the most direct sun. Think about whether you're likely to add an EV or battery in the next 3–5 years — this affects system design.

📄
Detailed & Transparent

You receive a detailed written quote

Within 1–3 business days of the site assessment, you'll receive a full written quote specifying every component of your system, all warranties, the installation timeline, and a clear breakdown of costs before and after rebates.

  • Exact panel make, model, wattage, and quantity
  • Exact inverter make and model
  • Racking and mounting system details
  • STC rebate clearly deducted from total
  • Installer workmanship warranty (minimum 5 years)
  • Estimated annual generation in kWh
  • Timeline from deposit to energisation

You decide — zero pressure, zero obligation

If the quote looks right, you're happy with the installer, and the numbers make sense for your home — great, let's go solar. If not, that's completely fine too. There is absolutely no obligation to proceed.

A quality installer earns your business by being transparent and thorough, not by creating urgency or pressure. If you ever feel pushed, that's a sign to walk away — and we'd want to know about it.

  • Take as long as you need to decide

🔧 After installation

Once you approve and a deposit is paid, the installer manages everything: ordering equipment, lodging the DNSP grid connection application, scheduling the installation crew, and commissioning the system. Most residential installs are completed in a single day. You'll receive your CEC compliance certificates, monitoring app setup, and a full handover.

Common questions about the process

Is SunReady Solar the installer?
No. We're an independent matching and education service. We connect you with local, CEC-accredited installers but we don't install panels ourselves. This means our advice is genuinely unbiased — we have no product or brand to push.
Will I get bombarded with calls?
No. We match you with one installer, not a list. You'll receive a single contact from the matched installer. We don't use your details for marketing, and we don't sell data to third parties.
How are installers selected for the network?
We only work with CEC-accredited installers who carry appropriate insurance, have a verifiable local presence, and maintain strong customer review scores. We monitor feedback and remove installers who don't meet our standards.
What if I want more than one quote?
We encourage it. Use our Quote Checklist to evaluate any additional quotes you get independently. Getting 2–3 quotes is good practice — just make sure you're comparing like for like.
I'm renting — can I still get a quote?
Solar installations require the property owner's consent and signature. If you're renting, we'd encourage you to speak to your landlord and share our guides with them — we can help owners of investment properties go solar too.
How long does the whole process take?
From submitting your details to solar being operational: typically 4–10 weeks. The assessment and quote usually happen within a week. Once you approve, the grid connection application (DNSP) takes 2–6 weeks in most states, and the installation itself is usually completed in a single day.
