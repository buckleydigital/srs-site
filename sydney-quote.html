Common Questions About Solar In Sydney

How much does solar cost in Sydney in 2026?
A quality 6.6kW solar system in Sydney typically costs $4,200–$6,800 after the federal STC rebate in 2026, depending on panel brand and installer. Use our free service to get a personalised quote for your home.
Is solar worth it in Sydney?
Absolutely. Sydney averages 4.6 peak sun hours daily. Most homeowners see a payback period of 3–5 years, with annual savings of $1,800–$2,800. Panel warranties run 25 years, meaning 20+ years of essentially free electricity.
What solar rebates are available in NSW?
NSW homeowners can access federal STCs (Small-scale Technology Certificates) which reduce upfront costs by $2,500–$3,500 on a typical 6.6kW system. Your installer handles the STC paperwork — the discount is applied at the point of sale.
Is this really free? What's the catch?
There's no catch. We may earn a referral fee from the installer if and when you choose to proceed — but only then, and it never affects your quoted price. The quote service is completely free with zero obligation.
Will I be hassled by sales calls?
No. Your matched installer contacts you once to provide a quote. If you're not interested, just say so — our network guidelines require them to respect that immediately.
How do you vet your Sydney installers?
Every installer must hold current CEC accreditation, carry appropriate insurance, have verifiable customer reviews, and meet our quality standards. We audit the network regularly and remove underperformers.
